Module 1 of 4

Understanding AI Limitations

AI tools can be incredibly useful, but they have important limitations. They can produce confident-sounding misinformation — a phenomenon called hallucination. This module explores how to use AI outputs critically and responsibly.

📄 Scenario: Research References

You're writing a paper on climate change. You ask an AI tool for academic sources and it gives you a list of 10 references — complete with author names, journal titles, and page numbers. What do you do?

💡
Key principle: AI does not know what it doesn't know. It can state false information with complete confidence. Always verify before you cite.
Module 2 of 4

Privacy & Data Protection

Protecting your personal information — and that of others — is crucial when using AI tools. What you share can be stored, reviewed, or used in ways you don't expect.

👥 Scenario: Study Group Help

You want to use AI to help plan a study group schedule. You need to include some participant details in your prompt to make it useful. What do you include?

⚠️
Never share: full names, addresses, student IDs, passwords, financial data, or sensitive research data with AI tools.
Module 3 of 4

Ethical Prompting

How you ask questions matters. Ethical prompting means asking for guidance and understanding — not outsourcing your thinking to the AI. The goal is to use AI as a tutor, not a ghostwriter.

✍️ Scenario: Essay Assignment

You have an essay due on the causes of World War I. You want to use AI to help. Which of these prompts is the most ethical and beneficial to your learning?

💡
Good prompts ask for explanations, outlines, examples, and feedback on your own work. Poor prompts ask AI to do the work for you.
Module 4 of 4

Academic Integrity

Understanding the line between ethical AI assistance and academic dishonesty is essential for your success and credibility as a student.

📝 Scenario: Take-Home Exam

You have a take-home exam where the instructions say "work must be your own." You find a question you're unsure about. What is the ethical approach?

⚠️
When in doubt, ask your instructor before using AI on any assessed work. Policies vary by course and assignment.

  • Always verify AI-generated information with credible sources.
  • Never share personal or sensitive data with AI tools.
  • Use AI to enhance your learning — not bypass it.
  • Follow your institution's specific AI policies for each course.
  • When in doubt, ask your instructor before using AI on assessed work.
