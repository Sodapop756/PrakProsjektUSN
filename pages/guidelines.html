AI Usage Guidelines

Clear rules and examples for responsible AI use in academic work

📚 Academic Integrity

Maintaining honesty and ethical standards while using AI tools.

DO

  • Use AI to explain concepts you don't understand
  • Ask for help with brainstorming and outlining
  • Request examples of good writing or problem-solving
  • Use AI to check your work for errors
  • Cite AI assistance when required by your institution

DON'T

  • Submit AI-generated work as your own
  • Use AI on exams or assignments where it's prohibited
  • Ask AI to write complete essays or solutions
  • Present AI ideas as your original thoughts without attribution
  • Use AI to bypass learning objectives

🛡️ Privacy & Data Protection

Protecting your personal information and sensitive data.

DO

  • Use generic examples instead of personal information
  • Assume AI conversations could be reviewed or stored
  • Check the privacy policy of AI tools before use
  • Use institutional AI tools when available (they may have better privacy protections)
  • Delete sensitive conversations when possible

DON'T

  • Share personal identifiers (name, student ID, address)
  • Input sensitive research data without permission
  • Share login credentials or financial information
  • Discuss private matters or confidential information
  • Assume AI providers won't use your data

🔍 Verification & Fact-Checking

Ensuring the accuracy of AI-generated information.

DO

  • Verify statistics and facts with credible sources
  • Check AI-generated references actually exist
  • Look for consensus among multiple reliable sources
  • Use academic databases and peer-reviewed journals
  • Consult with instructors about important information

DON'T

  • Assume AI is always correct
  • Use AI as a sole source of information
  • Trust AI-generated references without checking
  • Ignore contradictory information from reliable sources
  • Use AI for critical decisions without verification

🎯 Effective & Ethical Prompting

Asking questions that promote learning while maintaining integrity.

Ethical Prompting Checklist

Use this checklist when asking AI for help:

GOOD PROMPTS

  • "Explain the concept of photosynthesis in simple terms"
  • "Help me create an outline for an essay about climate change"
  • "What are common errors in solving quadratic equations?"
  • "Suggest strategies for effective time management"
  • "How can I improve the structure of this paragraph?"

POOR PROMPTS

  • "Write my 1000-word essay on Shakespeare"
  • "Solve these 10 calculus problems for me"
  • "Do my homework assignment about World War II"
  • "Generate a complete literature review for my paper"
  • "Answer these exam questions for my history test"

🏛️ Institutional Policies

Understanding and following your specific institution's rules.

Questions to Ask Your Institution:

  • • Is AI use allowed in this course/assignment?
  • • How should AI assistance be cited or acknowledged?
  • • Are there specific AI tools recommended or provided?
  • • What constitutes unacceptable AI use?
  • • Are there consequences for misuse of AI?

Important: These guidelines are general. Always follow your specific institution's policies, which may be more restrictive. When in doubt, ask your instructor or academic advisor.
