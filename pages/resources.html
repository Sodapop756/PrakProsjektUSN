Frequently Asked Questions

Is using AI considered cheating?
Using AI is not inherently cheating, but it can become cheating depending on how you use it. Using AI to enhance your learning, understand concepts, or get feedback is generally acceptable. Using AI to generate work that you submit as your own, especially without permission or proper citation, is typically considered academic dishonesty.
Do I need to cite AI-generated content?
Yes, in most cases. Most academic institutions require you to acknowledge AI assistance. The specific citation format varies, but typically you should mention: the AI tool used, the date of access, and your prompt. Always check your institution's specific guidelines.
How can I verify AI-generated information?
Always cross-reference important information with credible sources. Use academic databases, peer-reviewed journals, government publications, and reputable educational websites. Look for consensus among multiple reliable sources and check the references provided by AI.
What personal information should I never share with AI?
Never share: your full name, address, phone number, student ID, social security number, financial information, passwords, or any sensitive research data. Assume anything you input could be stored, reviewed, or used for training.
Can I use AI for exams or graded assignments?
This depends entirely on your institution's and instructor's policies. Some allow AI as a study aid but not for graded work. Some have specific guidelines for different types of assignments. When in doubt, always ask your instructor for clarification.
How can I use AI to actually learn, not just get answers?
Use AI as a tutor, not an answer machine. Ask for explanations of concepts, request analogies to help understanding, ask for practice problems, get feedback on your work, request outlines rather than full essays, and ask "why" questions to deepen your understanding.
